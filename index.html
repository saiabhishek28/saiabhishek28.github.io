Master’s Thesis

Influence of Discretization on Hypersampled Model Predictive Control

This thesis studies the capabilities of Hypersampled Model Predictive Control (HMPC), a new technique that aims to reduce the computational cost of MPC by changing how the optimal control problem is discretized. The thesis primarily focuses on understanding the influence of discretization on HMPC and experimenting with three distinct discretization methods (schemes). Each scheme is characterized by varying the discretization time at each step. These schemes influence how the controller predicts and reacts to changes, thereby affecting its overall performance. The experiments are designed to provide insights into how each method impacts the performance of HMPC, particularly in terms of accuracy, stability, and computational efficiency. The results are expected to be crucial in determining the most effective approach to implementing HMPC in various practical applications depending on system specifications.
